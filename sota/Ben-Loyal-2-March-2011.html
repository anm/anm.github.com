SOTA Activation: Ben Loyal, GM/NS-052

2 March 2011

Alistair MacLeod, MM0HAI

A brief report on a brief activation

Ben Loyal is FIXME: one of / the? most northerly mountains in Scotland. It's near Tongue. Loyal means level, which refers to the summit plateau. There are three main peaks; the highest and SOTA qualifying one is .... ...

We approached from the (FIXME) (NE?), passing the end peak. The path was poor and for the final section of climbing we just headed off up in the expected direction of the summit. After a while I saw some patches of snow and then rock through the mist. It tuned out we were in the right place; then the peak loomed.

The summit is a large slab. Initially it looked like there would bd a climb of moderate length over this. ALthough not at all steep this concerned me because the rock was wet and exposed. It would be easy to be blown over or slop and be blown over teh edge. It turned out that further round there was only a short climb to the summit.

The wind would have made it difficult and possibly dangerous to erect an HF antenna. It was also cold and unpleasant and I don't think the father would have tolerated the time taken for an HF activation. I almost did not actiate the summit at all but decided to try with the handie at least. I had to be careful to hold onto everything. There was quite a force on the aerial so I held that as well as the radio to save the connector. I think the uper sections of the aerial bent slightly.

Despite the remote location two contacts were made on 2m with stations in Stornoway and Elgin. The first was from sitting a few meters fro the trig point and the second with radio above trig and log paper on it. I managed to post a spot by SMS but there was no response - I probabably did not wait long enough. It was too cold. Operating zips to put the radio away was difficult. I think some very thin and tight gloves could be useful.

Descent was by closly retracing the ascent route, using boulders as memorized waypoints, until off the peak. There were no cliffs after this so it was simply a matter of pointing downhill until out of the mist.
